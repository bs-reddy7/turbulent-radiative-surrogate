# What Is a Turbulent Radiative Layer (TRL)?

## Definition
A turbulent radiative layer (TRL) is a region in a fluid where both turbulence and radiative heat transfer play important, coupled roles. These arise in astrophysical settings (stellar interiors, AGN disks) and high-energy-density physics.

## Key Physics
- **Turbulence**: chaotic fluid motion with a cascade of energy across scales
- **Radiation**: energy transport via photons, described by the radiative transfer equation
- **Coupling**: radiation can drive or damp turbulence; turbulence affects opacity and radiation fields

## Why It Is Hard to Simulate
- Requires resolving a wide range of spatial and temporal scales
- Radiation transport adds 6D integro-differential equations
- High computational cost motivates surrogate modeling
